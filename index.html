Maybe you've heard of mental conditions described as "chemical imbalances".
Sometimes, this is can be the case!
Similar to Goldilocks looking for a perfect bowl of porridge,
too much or too little of certain brain chemicals can cause undesired effects.
Brains want the "just-right" amount of chemicals to function properly!

Although we don't have all the answers of how the brain works,
we know that the brain and its networks are intricately connected.
All structures need to be in sync to work properly!
Due to the interconnectedness of the brain structures,
a drug meant to treat one condition can end up treating multiple other conditions.
...
In (very) simple terms, drugs target structures in the brain to produce its effects.
These drugs can affect brain structures, such as receptors, in different ways.
Here's an explanation for some of them:
Agonists
Binds to a receptor and causes the same actions as the substance that usually binds.
Antagonists
Block/oppose the actions of a receptor.
Inhibitors
Molecules that prevent normal enzyme activity.
Modulators
Alter receptor response by enhancing or reducing its actions.
However, there is no one magic mechanism!
